What is a Decentralized Identifier (DID)?

A DID is a new type of identifier that enables verifiable, decentralized digital identity. Unlike traditional federated identifiers, DIDs are designed to operate independently of centralized registries, identity providers, or certificate authorities.

Self-Sovereign Identity

You control your own identity. No reliance on third-party identity providers or central authorities.

Enhanced Security

Cryptographic security ensures that your identity cannot be tampered with or stolen.

Verifiable Credentials

Issue and verify claims about your identity that can be cryptographically verified by anyone.

How It Works

Register on our platform to create your unique Decentralized Identifier (DID). This generates cryptographic keys that you control, establishing your self-sovereign digital identity.

Enhance your security by adding biometric authentication. Our platform supports face recognition for quick and secure access to your digital identity.

Issue verifiable credentials to others or receive them from trusted entities. Each credential is cryptographically signed and can be verified by anyone without contacting the issuer.

Share your credentials with others via QR codes. Anyone can verify the authenticity of your credentials through our platform without compromising your privacy.
